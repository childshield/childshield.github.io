Background

In recent years, toys have evolved to acquire Internet connectivity and Artificial Intelligence (AI) capabilities. The physical toys and their connected systems have grown into an ecosystem referred to as Internet of Toys or IoToys.

The presence of sensors such as touch and motion detectors, microphones, cameras, etc. on IoToys has facilitated capture of children's data during setup and playtime. This coupled with data storage on the toy and/or cloud has resulted in safety concerns arising from collection, storage or theft of children's personal data.

Most IoToys appear as blackboxes to parents and do not clearly indicate the details of collection, storage or sharing of children's personal information. A visual cue in the form of a label can help parents make the right choices during purchase or usage of these toys.

What is the ChildShield Rating system?

The ChildShield rating system provides rubrics for calculating the Privacy and Security score of IoToys. This score is converted into an alphabetical rating from A to D that can be projected as a label on the IoToy packaging or website.

The ChildShield rating system can guide designers during the toy design phase to enable them make the right choices for children's safety. This can help to bring safer products in the market.

How do consumers (parents and children) benefit?

  • Parents want to buy safe products for children. The label assures parents that the product has been evaluated for privacy and security.
  • IoToys will cease to be black boxes to parents and they will get a better understanding of the product they are buying.

How do toy manufacturers benefit?

  • Manufacturers can demonstrate their commitment towards transparency and Internet safety of children.
  • They can tweak their designs to get better privacy and securty ratings.
  • The label can boost product visibility.
